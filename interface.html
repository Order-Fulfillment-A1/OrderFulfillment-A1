Site requirements

Project Description

Genko Oil has four departments: the Order Management Department (OD), the Supply Department (SD) including the Warehouse, the Carrier Department (CD), and the Finance Department (FD). It divided the Order Fulfillment process model into the Ordering, Carrier Appointment, Freight in Transit, and Payment.

Problem Identification

In the ordering subprocess, Genko Oil experienced difficulties in handling:

  • Transfer of data and information between Order Department and Supply Department in conducting PO approval.
  • PO Manager has difficulty in releasing the task being done to someone else if something happens.

In the Carrier Appointment sub-process section, Genko Oil experienced difficulties in handling:

  • Reallocation of tasks usually carried out by Junior Supply Officers.
  • Transferring data and information between workers and the Genko Oil department is quite challenging. In the Payment sub-process section, Genko Oil has difficulty in handling
  • Other staff find it difficult to synchronize their work with other staff.
  • It is difficult to see whether an order has been paid or not.

In the Freight in Transit sub-process, without a system, Genko Oil is difficult to handle:

  • Client questions about the whereabouts of the order.
  • Track orders.

In the Freight in Delivered sub-process section, without a system, Genko Oil and the client will find it difficult to handle:

  • Claim Return, Loss, or Damage Claim
  • Knowing whether the order has arrived.

Design Description


Every reason for our design is taken from our analysis on the Genko Oil problem case description. Our analysis is formed into these event lists based on every sub-process that happened in Genko Oil below
Ordering
  • OD Clerk (PO Manager or Client Liaison) creates Purchase Order (PO).
  • OD Clerk chose to reallocate the task to PO Manager.
  • PO Manager choose to relinquish working on the task and offer it to another PO Manager.
  • A PO Manager may suspend working on the creation of a PO and choose to resume working on it at some later stage.
  • A PO Manager may volunteer to be the primary entry point for processing POs during a certain period.
  • When a PO Manager is offered a task to create a PO, and they volunteer for it, the system will start the task automatically.
  • The PO Manager needs to decide which PO Manager will work on modification requests as they may occur at a later stage (default user is Carmine Marino).
  • PO Manager forwards the completed PO to Supply Department (SD) Officer.
  • If Supply Officer is Head of SD, they may choose to delegate this task to an SD clerk.
  • Supply Officers choose which approval tasks they will work on, and once they have chosen such a task, they may decide when to work on it.
  • Once a PO has been approved, Supply officer may request for modifications to PO Manager.
Carrier Appointmnet
  • Shipment Planner prepare the route guide
  • Shipment Planner estimate the trailer usage
  • Junior Supply Officers carry out a Prepare Transportation Quote
  • Junior Supply Officers Reallocate the tasks without losing previously completed work.
  • For shipments consisting of a single package :
    - Junior Supply Officer create a Motor Carrier Pickup manifest
    - Warehouse Admin Officer makes a Shipment Notice
  • For shipments that require a full truck load :
    - Client Liaisons arrange a Pickup appointment and a Delivery appointment.
    - Senior Supply Officer create a Shipment Information Document.
    - Warehouse Officer arrange Pick Up Appointment.
    - Junior Supply Officers set delivery appointments and modify delivery appointments if there are changes.
    - Warehouse Admin Officer makes a Shipment Notice.
  • For shipments that require a less than truck load :
    - Client Liaisons arrange a Pickup appointment and a Delivery appointment
    - Senior Supply Officer make a Bill of Lading
    - Warehouse Officer arrange Delivery Appointment
    - Junior Supply Officers set delivery appointments and modify delivery appointments if there are changes.
    - Warehouse Admin Officer makes a Shipment Notice
Payment
  • Supply admin officer creates Shipment Invoice.
  • PO Manager specifying whether the payment is done in advance or not.
  • Finance officer issuing a Shipment Remittance Advice if payment was done in advance.
  • Finance officer issuing a Shipment payment order if there was no payment in advance.
  • Finance officer seeking approval for the shipment payment order document.
  • Senior finance officer approving the Shipment Payment Order document.
  • Finance officers decide whether they need to modify the shipment payment order document or finalize it.
  • Client made payment.
  • Account manager starts processing payment for shipment by specifying shipment cost.
  • Account manager checks the client payment to see if the client is paying less or more.
  • Account manager creates a debit adjustment, sends notification to the client, and reprocesses the payment if the client pays less.
  • Account manager creates credit adjustment and sends notification to the client if the client pays more.
  • Supply admin officer creates Freight Invoice if PO Manager specified that freight Invoice is needed.
Freight in Transit
  • Courier issuing Trackpoint Notice
  • Carrier Admin Officer check trackpoint visits
  • Carrier Admin Officer create Log Trackpoint Order Entry
  • Client Liaison check logged entries
  • Client Liaison initiate Shipment Status Inquiry
  • Purchase Order Manager check delivery completion
  • Purchase Order Manager create Acceptance Certificate
Freight in Delivered
  • Client Liaison read customer request
  • Approve Purchase Order
  • Client Liaison check Timeout
  • Client Liaison create document for Return Merchandise
  • Client Liaison create document for Loss or Damage Claim
  • Client Liaison submit documents
  • Senior Supply Officer accept document submission
  • Senior Supply Officer authorize document for Return Merchandise
  • Senior Supply Officer authorize document for Loss or Damage Claim
